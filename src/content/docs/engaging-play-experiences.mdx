Our focus for this workstream is on moving away from a uniform asphalt expanse towards a **Learning Landscape**. We are looking for play solutions that go beyond simple entertainment, focusing instead on physical development, managed risk, and social cooperation.

## A Zoned Approach

One of the strongest pieces of feedback from our survey was the need for better "Zoning." We are evaluating how to divide the space to ensure that different types of play can happen simultaneously without conflict.

## Reflecting Community Priorities

The results from **The Playground Shop** have given us a clear indication of what the Viewlands community values most. We are using this data to guide our research and our conversations with professional designers.

### High-Energy Sport & Agility

With **Football Targets (66 tokens)**, **Basketball (40 tokens)**, and the **Running Track (39 tokens)** performing so strongly, we are evaluating:

- **Dedicated Sports Surfaces:** Including the long-term potential for a MUGA (Multi-Use Games Area) that could serve both the school and the wider community.
- **The Daily Mile:** Identifying the best permanent route for a track to support school health and fitness goals.

### Collaborative & Social Play

We are prioritising equipment that multiple children can use together to promote social cooperation rather than competition.

- **Group Swings:** Following high interest in the **Log/Rope Swing (29 tokens)**, we are looking for items that encourage kids to work together.
- **Monkey Bars & Agility:** With **41 tokens** spent on Monkey Bars, there is a clear demand for equipment that provides a physical challenge.

## Design Principles

As we evaluate different equipment and layouts, we are guided by three core principles:

### 1. Managed Risk

We believe in providing "challenging play" where children can test their limits and build resilience. This includes exploring how to safely utilise the natural topography of the **grass slope** for climbing or sliding features.

### 2. Physical Development

We are looking for equipment that promotes a wide range of movement—running, balancing, hanging, and climbing—to support agility and confidence.

### 3. Inclusive Play Opportunities

A core consideration is ensuring that the "play value" is accessible to all. We are identifying ways to ensure that children of all abilities find the playground engaging.
