This workstream focuses on the physical _bones_ of the playground. By improving the natural and built environment, we aim to soften the sparse, exposed nature of the site to create a space that is comfortable, safe, and durable.

## Our Ecological Vision

Viewlands is currently an asphalt-heavy site that offers limited biodiversity and few opportunities for children to engage with the natural world. Our vision is to transform these grounds into a _living landscape_ that fosters environmental stewardship.

By introducing native planting and establishing dedicated zones for growing food, we can provide vital opportunities for pupils to learn about food systems and the environment first-hand. This resilient environment will prioritise the use of reclaimed materials to minimise our ecological footprint and create a sustainable legacy for the school.

---

## Addressing Site-Specific Challenges

Our approach to landscaping is shaped by the unique and demanding conditions of the Viewlands grounds.

### 1. Weather & Shelter

Being at the _top of the hill_ leaves the playground exposed to the elements. We are exploring the introduction of shelter and weather protection to help pupils and staff escape the wind and rain that often funnel across the site.

### 2. Mud & Surface Management

Deteriorating grass and mud build-up are major issues during wet weather. We are investigating the suitability of woodchip for the most affected areas and exploring how to safely utilise the natural topography of the _grass slope_ for low-risk features, provided ground conditions can be managed.

### 3. Security & Longevity

Given that the Viewlands nursery has suffered from vandalism in the past, all _new-build_ elements must be designed for durability. We are focused on using vandal-resistant materials and ensuring layouts maintain clear lines of sight for supervision.

### 4. Respecting our Neighbours

Any new landscaping or fencing will be carefully considered to ensure it does not negatively impact the privacy, light, or views of those living adjacent to the school.

We would like to acknowledge the significant support provided by the **Westbank Project**. Their contributions have had an immediate visible impact:

- **Equipment Removal:** The safe removal of broken playground items.
- **Sustainable Seating:** The installation of heavy-duty picnic benches created from reclaimed timber. These were selected for being suitably heavy and difficult to move, ensuring they remain durable and secure.
